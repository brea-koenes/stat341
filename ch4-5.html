4.14

  1. In the Beta-Binomial setting, show that we can write the posterior mode of pi as the weighted average of the prior mode and observed sample success rate:

$$ \[\begin{align*} Mode(\piY=y) &= (\alpha+y1)/(\alpha+\beta+n2) \\ &= \frac{\alpha1}{\alpha+\beta+n2} + y/(\alpha+\beta+n2) \\ &= (\alpha1)/(\alpha+\beta+n2) (\alpha+\beta2)/(\alpha+\beta2) + y/(\alpha+\beta+n2) n/n \\ &= (\alpha+\beta2)/(\alpha+\beta+n2) (\alpha1)/(\alpha+\beta2) + n/(\alpha+\beta+n2) y/n \\ &= (\alpha+\beta2) / (\alpha+\beta+n2) Mode(\pi) + n/(\alpha+\beta+n2) y/n \\ \end{align*}\] $$

  1. To what value does the posterior mode converge as our sample size n increases? Support your answer with evidence.

{w1, w2,} consists of a countable (possibly infinite) set of values

$$

\lim{n \infty} Pr(w_t|x_1,x_n)=1, \ \lim{n \infty} Pr(w_i|x_1,x_n)=0, \ i!=t

$$

Using the strong law of large numbers and given that there are appropriate regularity conditions, it can be shown that the posterior converges to 1 as n increases.
